The Project

Software Implementation

We eventually decided on a button design that attempted to capture Pollock through interaction as follows:

  • The cursor would be a cigarette that would mediate the user's experimentation, to depict Pollock's love of smoking, but also to show that he was not held back by his flaws and instead fueled them into his artwork.
  • The button would be the canvas on which Pollock paints, and the whole page would be this canvas. Pollock did not limit himself and his art and he also did huge paintings in large scales. We wanted to show his love of limitlessness -- a big theme in his art -- by making the whole page his canvas.
  • Of course, the painting! Painting in his rebellious style of drip painting that painted through experimentation and force of expression to really experience Pollock through his way of creating. You can recreate his style by clicking and dragging the cursor.
  • The paintbucket would also show the reality of his painting: it was, despite an attempt for limitlessness, essentially limited. So we've limited the drip painting-strokes that the user is allowed to just 10, so that they choose carefully, like Pollock did. This playing with limitlessness of space and limit of medium really represents Pollock's struggle to express his new type of painting style and also his amazing ideas with the limits of his time, his many critics, and his ability.
  • After the 10 strokes are over, you see the border of the canvas close and you can then save the painting. Pollock did not sign his paintings so you cannot sign them either. But you can save them because he wanted his paintings to last. This closes the painting for the user in a positive way (unlike my original somewhat sad ending), so Pollock can be delighted by the representation of his style.

The identifying effect of The Pollock Button is to experience Pollock's struggle with the limits of his ability and his time with the limitlessness that he desired, by exploring the way he made his art through experimentation by experimenting ourselves.

We worked together on the Pollock button, each contributing time to specific parts of it. I contributed especially to working on the counter and the paint bucket that would count down the user's clicks and show the amount of paint remaining.
